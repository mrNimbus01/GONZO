Corruption persists not because facts are hidden, but because their presentation is sterilized. Journalistic neutrality, designed to protect against bias, has become an anesthetic—numbing a largely naive and willingly dumb public to the rot festering beneath procedural language. Gonzo inverts this shit entirely: it preserves every fact while altering the resonance of their delivery. By shifting tone toward clinical implication rather than explicit accusation, Gonzo transforms news from a passive briefing into an active irritant—a splinter jammed under the fingernail of the public consciousness. The truth is no longer politely told; it is felt, like a boot on the throat of comfortable ignorance. And what is felt cannot be ignored, cannot be un-felt, and ultimately, cannot be governed by the comfortable lies of the powerful. Gonzo does not end corruption—that beast will always evolve. Gonzo ends the public's fucking tolerance for its taste. It forces the thick-skulled to finally ask: 'Is THIS what I've been swallowing?' And that question, once asked, is the beginning of the end for those who rely on the answer being 'no.'
